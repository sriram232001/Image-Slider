The vibration of this sound is sometimes referred to as a divine principle or the origin of all creation

Shivan

The concept of Lord Shiva being "ready" typically refers to his eternal presence and constant availability to his devotees. He is a compassionate and loving deity, always ready to help the needy and embrace them regardless of their past actions.

This readiness can also be understood as the state of spiritual readiness within a devotee to receive his grace through compassion, forgiveness, and surrender to Him


Shri Rama

"Vinayaka" refers to Lord Ganesha, the elephant-headed deity in Hinduism. "God ready" likely means you're preparing for a festival or ceremony honoring him, such as Ganesh Chaturthi (also called Vinayaka Chaturthi in Tamil Nadu).

To get "ready," you might prepare puja items like flowers, incense, and sweets, set up a Ganpati idol made of clay, and invite his blessings for wisdom and the removal of obstacles.


Murugan

The phrase "Murugan god ready" most likely refers to a devotee's desire to worship or connect with Lord Murugan, a Hindu deity known as the God of War, Victory, and Wisdom. This expression signifies a person's readiness to pray, offering their devotion and trust to the deity for his blessings in overcoming difficulties and achieving success.

The readiness is often symbolized by performing rituals, chanting mantras, or attending festivals dedicated to Murugan, such as Skanda Sashti.


Krishna

"Krishna god ready" is likely referring to preparing for the Hindu festival of Krishna Janmashtami, which celebrates the birth of Lord Krishna.

It can also refer to getting ready to dress up as Krishna for a celebration, or preparing a space with Krishna-related decor.


Hanuman

The term "Hanuman god ready" likely refers to the depiction of Lord Hanuman in a ready or powerful stance, often seen in artworks and statues, showcasing his strength, devotion, and readiness to act, such as protecting and serving Lord Rama.

The concept of Hanuman being "ready" reflects his character as a divine warrior and protector who embodies courage and steadfast loyalty.
